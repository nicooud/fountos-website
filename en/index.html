The problem

Your work lives in seven places. None of them is calm.

Notes app, Notion, voice memos, that one spreadsheet, your head. Every tool promised order. Every tool added a feed. You don't need more AI shouting suggestions — you need to find the thing you wrote last Tuesday.

The Lens

One folder. Many lenses.

A Lens is a way of looking. Point one at a folder and it becomes a task list, a journal, a client file. Your files don't move, don't convert, don't get trapped — they stay plain markdown on your disk. The Lens is just how you see them.

How it works

Plain files. Local first. AI optional.

Your files stay files.

Markdown on your disk. Readable in any editor, today and in ten years.

Everything runs on your device.

No account required to start. No cloud in the loop.

AI, if you want it.

An optional local colleague that filters and remembers. Turn it off and Fount still works — completely.

Privacy
On-device

Your files never leave your device.

No telemetry. No analytics inside the app. No training on your data. We can't see your files — and we built it that way, so you never have to take our word for it.

Built in the EU. Privacy by architecture, not by policy.

Why I'm building this

I ran my company out of notes apps, voice memos, and my own head. I didn't need another AI to generate more — I needed something to protect my attention. So I built it, file by file, on my own machine. Fount is that system, made for you.

Nico Oud
Founder
