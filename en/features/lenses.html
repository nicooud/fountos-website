Features  ·  Lenses
Lenses

Same files. A clearer view.

A Lens points at a folder you already have and shows it as a task list, a person, a day. The files never move — a Lens is a way of looking, not a container.

Join the waitlist
The objection

"Won't I be locked in again?" No. A Lens is just a way of looking. Your files don't move, don't convert, and aren't copied into anything. Turn a Lens off and your folder is exactly as it was.

How it works

Read. Reveal. Nothing moves.

01

Your files stay put.

A Lens reads your plain markdown in place — it never moves, renames, or duplicates anything.

02

Choose a view.

Point a Lens at a folder and it assembles a view — tasks, people, a day — from what's already written.

03

Switch freely.

Many Lenses, one set of files. Open the same notes in any editor tomorrow; they were never ours.

The detail that earns trust

Your files don't move.

A Lens is a read, not a container. There's no Fount format, no import step, and nothing to export — because the files were always plain markdown on your disk.
