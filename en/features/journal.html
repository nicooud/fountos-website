Journal

What you were thinking that week — findable a year later.

Journal reads the notes you already write and arranges them by time, so a stretch of thinking stays whole and searchable later.

Join the waitlist
The objection

"I won't keep it up anyway." You don't have to keep anything up. Journal reads the notes you already write; it's a way to look back, not another habit to maintain.

How it works

Write freely. Look back later.

01

Write when you write.

No streak, no daily prompt, no guilt for the gaps.

02

It arranges by time.

Your entries line up by day and week, so a stretch of thinking stays whole.

03

Findable later.

Ask what you were working through last spring; the answer is a note you can open.

The detail that earns trust

No streak to break.

There's no counter and nothing to lose. Journal is plain markdown arranged by date — miss a week and nothing scolds you.
