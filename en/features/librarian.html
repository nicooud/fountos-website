The objection

"Not another system to maintain." That's exactly the point: you don't maintain this one. There's no schema to design, no tags to invent, no weekend lost in settings. The Librarian does the organising so the tidying never becomes your second job.

How it works

Read. Propose. Only then file.

01

Point it at your folder.

Nothing moves, nothing uploads. Fount reads your plain markdown in place, exactly where it already lives.

02

It reads, and proposes.

The Librarian suggests where things belong and surfaces what connects — as proposals you can glance at, not changes you have to police.

03

You approve; it files.

Nothing changes until you say so. Approve a suggestion and it's done; ignore it and nothing happens.

The detail that earns trust

Propose-then-apply, always with a source.

The Librarian never rewrites your files behind your back. Every move is a proposal first — it shows you the change and the exact note it came from, then waits for your yes. Nothing is invented, nothing is hidden, and the answer always points back to a file you can open.
