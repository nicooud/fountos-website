Fount OS  ·  Privacy

Your files never leave your device.

Built in the EU by a privacy-first founder

The short version: your files are yours, and we never see them. Fount OS has no servers, no accounts, no analytics, and no telemetry. We can't read, collect, or sell your files — because they never reach us.

This page explains, in plain language, what Fount touches, where it lives, and the few situations where anything can leave your device — always at your explicit choice.

1 · Your files live on your device

Everything you keep in Fount — notes, documents, tasks, links, attachments, and settings — is stored as plain markdown files and folders on your own device, in a location you choose. There is no Fount server, no cloud copy managed by us, and no hidden database elsewhere.

Because it's just files on your disk:

· You can open, back up, move, export, or delete them at any time, with or without Fount.
· Deleting the app or the folder removes your data — there's nothing for us to delete on your behalf, because we never had it.
· We have no technical ability to access, read, restore, or recover your files. That's by design.

2 · iCloud sync (optional)

If you choose to keep your folder in iCloud Drive, your files sync between your devices using your own Apple iCloud account. In that case:

· The sync relationship is between you and Apple. Apple processes your files as your service provider under your iCloud agreement and Apple's privacy policy — not as a provider to Fount.
· Fount has no access to your iCloud account or to the files synced through it.
· iCloud encryption and data-location details are governed by your Apple account settings, including Advanced Data Protection if you enable it.

If you keep your folder outside iCloud Drive, nothing syncs anywhere.

3 · No telemetry, no analytics, no accounts

Fount contains no analytics or tracking SDKs — we don't measure how you use the app. No telemetry: the app doesn't phone home, and we collect no usage data, crash fingerprints, device identifiers, or IP addresses. No accounts: there's no sign-up, no login, no email list tied to the app, and no user database. No advertising, and no data to share with advertisers.

If you choose to share crash reports or diagnostics through the settings of your Apple device, Apple may forward anonymized crash data to us under Apple's own opt-in mechanism. That's controlled entirely by your Apple settings, not by Fount.

4 · On-device AI

Fount uses Apple Foundation Models for AI features such as summarizing, linking, and organizing your notes. These models run entirely on your device:

· Your content is processed locally, in memory, on your own hardware.
· Nothing is uploaded, logged, or stored outside your device for these features.
· No AI provider — including Apple, and including us — receives your content through the on-device AI features.

If your device doesn't support on-device models, the related features are simply unavailable. Your files are never silently routed elsewhere as a fallback.

5 · Optional connections you can turn on

Fount is fully functional without any external connection. One capability can send data off your device — only if you explicitly turn it on, and you can turn it off again at any time.

External AI providers via MCP. You can optionally connect Fount to an external AI service (for example, Anthropic's Claude) using the Model Context Protocol (MCP). If you set up such a connection:

· Content is sent to that provider only when you actively use the connection, and only the content involved in that request.
· The provider processes your data under its own terms and privacy policy, using your own account or API key. Fount is not a party to that relationship and doesn't sit in the middle — data travels directly from your device to the provider.
· Disconnecting the provider stops all data flow immediately. Nothing is retained by Fount, because nothing passed through Fount.

We recommend reading the privacy policy of any provider before you connect it.

6 · App Store and Apple

You get Fount through Apple's App Store. Your purchase, payment details, App Store account, and app updates are handled by Apple, under Apple's terms and privacy policy. We receive aggregated, anonymized sales statistics from Apple, which contain no personal data. The App Store privacy label for Fount reflects this policy: Data Not Collected.

7 · Children

Fount is a productivity tool and is not directed at children under 16. We don't knowingly collect data from anyone — including children — because the app collects no data at all. If you're a parent and have questions, contact us at mail@nicooud.com.

8 · Your rights

Under the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and similar laws, you have rights over personal data an organization processes about you — access, correction, deletion, and portability. Our position is simple: we don't process your personal data on any server, and we hold no records about you. Your files are under your exclusive control, which means:

· Access & portability: your data is already in your hands, in open markdown files.
· Correction & deletion: edit or delete your files directly; no request to us is needed or possible.
· Data we hold about you: none — unless you email us, in which case we use your email address and message solely to reply to you (legal basis: our legitimate interest in answering you, Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR), and for nothing else.

If you believe we've handled your data improperly, contact us at mail@nicooud.com, or lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority (in the Netherlands: the Autoriteit Persoonsgegevens).

9 · Changes to this policy

If Fount ever changes in a way that affects your privacy — for example, a new optional feature that can send data off your device — we'll update this policy before the change ships, update the "Last updated" date above, and describe the change in the app's release notes. We'll never enable off-device data flows silently or retroactively.
