Fount OS vs Notion

A cloud database is not your files.

When Notion is the better choice: if you're running a shared team knowledge base — relational databases, wikis, collaborative docs with permissions — Notion is built for exactly that, and it's excellent at it. Fount isn't a team database, and doesn't pretend to be.

The difference in one paragraph

Notion is a superb cloud workspace. But it's a database in someone else's cloud, not plain files on your disk — and for the contracts, client notes, and numbers a founder can't paste into a cloud, that difference is the whole game. Fount keeps the same information as markdown you own, and lets an on-device AI actually work on it.

A cloud database is not your files.

Bringing your notes over? Your files come as they are. No import, no conversion, no waiting — point Fount at the folder and it reads them in place.
